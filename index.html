How we figure out your value

We look at four things that brands care about. We mix them together to get your number.

Your fans

How many people follow you, and how many like and comment. A small, active fan base can beat a big, quiet one.

Common questions

What is NIL?

NIL means Name, Image & Likeness. Since 2021, college athletes (and high schoolers in many states) can get paid for ads, social posts, autographs and more. They don't lose their spot on the team.

How right is this number?

It's a smart estimate, not a real offer. We use the same things brands look at. Real deals can be higher or lower. Use it as a starting point.

Why do likes matter more than followers?

Brands pay for fans who pay attention. A small page where lots of people like and comment can be worth more than a big page where no one does.

Can high school athletes earn NIL money?

Yes. Many states let high schoolers do NIL deals. Local shops, camps and gear brands are common partners.

Do you save my info?

Your answers stay on your device. We only get your email if you ask for your full report. See our Privacy Policy.
